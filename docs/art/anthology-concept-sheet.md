## Final prompt

> Design one unmistakably art-directed pixel-zine anthology sheet with exactly
> ten miniature game scenes, arranged as irregular pasted screen-print panels
> in two loose rows of five with quiet cream negative space. Derive the visual
> grammar from the supplied website reference: cream paper, near-black ink,
> hot-pink annotation marks, butter-yellow highlighter, baby-blue and pale-pink
> paper scraps, bold geometric black shapes, typewriter texture, visible tape,
> halftone dots, photocopy grain, and slightly crooked hand-assembled spacing.
> Do not copy its logo, portrait, wording, or exact layout. Show: an angular
> faceted crystal Mote beside a spacecraft music console; a human courier with
> one parcel on a curved orbital walkway; a mechanic repairing one friendly
> wheeled utility robot; a lone radio operator tuning a ghostly signal; a lunar
> skiff luring a constellation creature; a sparse grid tactics standoff; a field
> researcher observing one gentle giant creature; an adventurer rotating a
> stone chamber around a key; three distinct obsolete delivery robots racing at
> sunset; and a witch placing beetle-shaped software familiars on a circuit
> board. Combine hard-edged 8-by-8 pixel clusters with 1970s photocopied zine
> collage and two-to-five-color risograph printing. Use flat screen-printed
> shapes, sparse one-pixel highlights, imperfect registration, torn-paper or
> pinked-edge frames, tape, underlines, circles, and small arrows. Make every
> panel use a different silhouette language; avoid repeating heads, eyes,
> chassis, poses, lighting, or camera angles. Use cream `#f5f0e6` and near-black
> `#1a1a1a` with limited hot pink `#ff6b9d`, butter yellow `#ffe566`, baby blue
> `#a8d5e5`, and pale pink `#f4c7c3`. No text, pseudo-writing, logos, commercial
> characters, generic cube-headed robots, repeated cyan eyes, painterly pixels,
> glossy rendering, perfect symmetry, uniform cinematic lighting, clutter,
> malformed limbs, fused hands, or watermarks.
